Therapy

Therapy, for me, is not a path of “fixing” – it is a return. Just as the tea ceremony is not about the tea itself, but about meeting. About stillness. About respect for the present moment.

In my therapeutic practice I focus primarily on children, adolescents, and women – groups that often carry the greatest vulnerability.

I work eclectically, according to the unique needs of each person, drawing on effective tools from various psychotherapeutic approaches (biosynthesis, SFT, DBT, and others), including non-verbal methods such as art therapy, play therapy, and sandtray work. I also integrate current insights from neurobiology into my practice.

I believe that a conversation held in an atmosphere of trust, calm, and natural presence can open a path toward inner balance and personal growth. For this reason, I work exclusively in person, with trust in the power of genuine human connection.

A cup of warm tea here is not just an addition – it is a symbol of slowing down, quieting the mind, and care.

Who I Work With

Children who need understanding and a sense of safety in a changing world.

Adolescents who are searching for themselves and their place, and who need to be accepted as they are.

Women who long for a space of their own – for calm, clarity, or change.

Sessions take place in the calm and safe environment of my Therapy Studio (see photos), over a cup of organic tea of your choice (including fruit and herbal teas for children), through sensitive and supportive conversation or non-verbal techniques, always aligned with your needs, goals, and individual pace.
